<h2 align="center">A retro-style dungeon crawler developed for the Shiba Game Jam, where it reached the top 30 out of 5,000 participants.</h2>

### **Ever wonder what the life of an adventuring, slightly schizophrenic crab is like? I did.**

Thus, for the Shiba Game Jam by Hack Club, I made Cavern Crabber, a fast-paced, action-based progression game where the player, a crab, must battle their way through the Dungeon of Decay, finding the memories of their old life as they make their way to the bottom-most floor.

Made for (and somehow won) the Shiba Game Jam by Hack Club.

## Gameplay
Control an amnesiac crab through an eerie, decaying dungeon.

Advance past mutated enemies, deadly traps, and environmental hazards to reach next floor of the dungeon.

Explore the ecosytems in each level, and do your best to survive without losing yourself to your old memories.

Finale is a WIP, though it should be complete by late 2026.

## Themes

The game found inspiration from the cliche trope of [Lost Pet Grievance](https://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/LostPetGrievance)--rather of following the tried-and-true format, Cavern Crabber decided to flip that on its head. What if the **pet** lost their owner? With that, Cavern Crabber was born.

From there, a myriad of features--all wrapped in hilarious dialogue and a silly style--came into play, including:
- Memory reveals each level, uncovering the backstory of our beloved crab.
- Moral dilemmas--are we really the good guys?
- And a slow questioning of, "Who am I, and what will I be?"

## Project Structure

Key folders include: 

`Scenes/` ~ Contains all the Godot scenes relevant to the game.

`Scripts/` ~ Contains all the GDScript logic controlling game behavior.

`Sprites/` ~ Contains all the visual assets (crab, enemies, environment, etc).

`Audio/` ~ Contains all the sound effects and music.

`addons/` ~ Configuration files relevant to Godot.
